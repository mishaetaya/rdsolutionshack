Inaugural R&D Solutions Hackathon!

Are you up for it? Learn More below

+What is it?

A four day hackathon to prototype ideas for some of Elsevier's biggest challenges. You will work in groups of 3-4 persons, and the event will be spread across two weekends (no need to take days off school or work).

The objective will be to:

  • Determine a solution (day 1)
  • Prototype the solution/experience (day 2)
  • Pitch the idea to a panel of Senior Elsevier Executives and Industry experts (day 3)
+ What's in it for you?
  • Earn Prizes - 1st and 2nd place teams will receive a cash prize
  • Learn about real world challenges with Big Data in some of our most cirtical industries such as Pharmaceuticals, Oil & Gas, Chemicals and others
  • Work closely with Senior Elsevier Executives – including Head of Strategy and BD, CTO, CMO, as well as interact with other senior members of the Management Team
  • Build your resume and experience by putting strategy into practice
  • ...and of course as much free pizza, drinks and other snacks as you need to keep you going throughout the day :)

The Hackathon will focus on the below three topics

You can only choose one topic to work

Awareness in the Academic Community

Help us drastically increase awareness of Elsevier R&D tools in the Academic community. We are on a journey to improve how research is done, but the first step is getting the word out.



What would you do to make sure every professor, student and researcher knows who we are, what we do and thinks of us first when they need research help?

Safety Challenges in the Chemicals Industry

Build the next generation Big Data solution for the Chemicals industry. Our product and markting teams have spent several months speaking with Chemicals executives, scientists and engineers to identify their biggest pain points. Now, we need to take what we learned and solve for those challenges.

Can you prototype a user experience that addresses the Chemicals companies Big Data challenges?

Scientific Information for the Internet of Things

Inter of Things is the latest buzz ... so much as Big Data is getting jealous. Elsevier is the biggest scientific publisher of data across many disciplines, and we have databases of data easily accessibly API's, and it can have a huge impact on how real world data is interpreted

Can you define a use case for scientific data, and prototype a user experience for a customer in either the Chemicals, Pharma or Oil & Gas industry?
