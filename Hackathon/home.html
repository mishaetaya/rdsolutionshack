>What is it?

A three day hackathon to prototype ideas for some of Elsevier's biggest challenges. You will work in groups of 3-4 persons, starting on a Friday and ending on Sunday (no need to take days off school or work).

The objective will be to:

  • Friday (Day 1) - Kick off, form into teams, mingle, learn about Elsevier and enjoy drinks and snacks
  • Saturday (Day 2) - Start designing, building, testing, etc.
  • Sunday (Day 3) - Finalize prototyping and Demo Day!
> What's in it for you?
  • Earn Prizes - 1st place gets $1500, 2nd place gets $1000 and 3rd place will receive $500 cash prize
  • Learn about real world Big Data challenges in Pharmaceuticals, Oil & Gas, Chemicals and other industries
  • Play with data normally only available to our customers
  • Work closely with Senior Elsevier Executives – including Head of Strategy, CTO, CMO, and other senior executives
  • Build your resume and experience by putting strategy into practice
  • Potentially land a job - our goal is to put ideas into action and need people that can turn an idea into a campaign, a product or a business
  • ...and of course as much free pizza, drinks and other snacks as you need to keep you going throughout the day :)
> When is this happening?

The event will be held on January 22-24, 2016 at Elsevier's Knovel office on 37th street and 7th Avenue

The Hackathon will focus on the below four topics

Choose one topic

Awareness in the Academic Community

Help us drastically increase awareness of Elsevier R&D tools in the Academic community. We are on a journey to improve how research is done, but the first step is getting the word out.

What would you do to make sure every professor, student and researcher knows who we are, what we do and thinks of us first when they need research help?

Attracting the best talent in a tech savvy world

We're doing a lot of cutting edge work at Elsevier on topics of Big Data, Drug Discovery, Natural Language Processing, Data Mapping and many more topics. Problem is...not enough people know this.

Can you create a viral marketing campaign to jump start awareness in the millenial community or a tool that helps us engage young technologists, marketers and product developers in a unique and fun way?

Scientific Information for the Internet of Things

Internet of Things is the latest buzz ... even Big Data is getting jealous. Elsevier is the biggest scientific publisher of data across many disciplines, and we have databases of data easily accessibly API's, and it can have a huge impact on how real world data is interpreted

Can you define a use case for scientific data, and prototype a user experience for a customer in either the Chemicals, Pharma or Oil & Gas industry?

Social Media in Pharmaceutical Industry

Drug safety is a priority use case for Elsevier - we help Pharma companies monitor adverse events of drugs in published literature. However social media presents a much bigger pool of data that Pharma companies haven't learned how to deal with yet.

Can you define a use case for how pharmaceutical companies might use social media data to monitor drug safety, and prototype a quick app?
